Build. Fly. Crash. Repeat.

Jansen Hillis

12/3/2020

In 2013, I decided, on a whim, to buy a toy quadcopter and see what all the fuss was about. A few days later, my first toy drone arrived - a Walkera Ladybird v2.

This particular toy was barebones. Nothing special, just a few parts, and it came preassembled:

  • A basic flight controller board
  • 4x tiny brushed motors
  • 4x tiny orange and black propellors
  • 1x 3.7V 250mAh Lithium Ion (LiPo) Battery
  • A plastic fuselage shroud (for protection))
  • Extra props and chassis accessories

These little toys are excellent to learn on. One thing is for sure, crashing should be expected. Part of the fun is picking up the pieces, performing repairs, changing the design, and flying again! Because batteries are still rather a poor energy storage solution for these type of aircraft, a single flight with these toys might only last 6-8 minutes at a time! I started buying more batteries to increase the number of flights I could do consecutively. While I was flying one battery, 4 more were rapidly charging away.
